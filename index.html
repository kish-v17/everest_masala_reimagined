SPICES

India's spices are a symphony for the senses. From the earthy warmth of turmeric to the bright citrus zing of cardamom, each adds a unique note to a dish. These fragrant treasures transform simple ingredients into culinary masterpieces, while boasting health benefits that have been revered for centuries. A sprinkle of spice is all it takes to transport your taste buds on a vibrant journey through India.

Products You Will Love


BLENDED SPICES

Nothing can beat the delicious taste of traditional Indian spice blends! Everest’s range of blended spices are hand-crafted & the ingredients are sourced from India’s renowned spice lands, ensuring the taste, aroma, flavour & color remain uncompromised.


PURE SPICES

Pure spices play an integral part in Indian cuisine irrespective of the zone, state, community or the cooking method and hence occupies a very special place of honor. We, at Everest, believe in offering spices that are rich & authentic. And nothing more speaks of this belief than our range of Pure Spices.

History of Everest

1967
Shri Vadilal Bhai Shah registered the brand Everest and launched first 3 products in the market – Milk Masala, Garam Masala & Tea Masala.
1981
Vadibhai’s son Sanjeev Shah joins the organization and works toward building the sales and distribution of the company.
1982
Everest held its first dealer meet ‘Everest Ki Yadgaar Raat’ to promote the brand and create awareness
1983
Everest created their first television commercial with producer Harish Bhimani and started broadcasting on Doordarshan. In the same year, Everest buys their First Small Factory In Shiv Krupa Industrial Estate In Vikhroli, Mumbai.
1992
Vadibhai’s Second Son Rajiv Shah Joins The Business.
1996
To Meet The Growing Demands, Everest Opens The Second Factory In Mumbai At Kurla Industrial Estate.
2000
Everest Spices Inaugurates Its Third Factory In Vikhroli, Mumbai.
2002
Everest launches first blended chilli powder under the brand name Tikhalal
2003
Everest Won The First Superbrand Award And Has Continued To Achieved ‘Superbrand’ Status Eight Times (2003, 2006, 2009, 2012, 2015, 2017, 2019 & 2021)
2007
Everest becomes India’s leading Spice brand as per A.C. Nielsen with product range of more than 45 flavours.
2010
Everest exports spices to more than 65 countries.
2014
Everest launched Hing under the brand Hingraj.
2016
Everest Introduces Kanda Lahsun Masala & Ropes In Ashok Saraf As Brand Ambassador.
2017
Indian Film Legend Amitabh Bachchan Becomes The Brand Ambassador Of Everest Spices. In the same year, Everest Spices Inaugurates the Largest Plant In Umbergaon, Gujarat.
2018
Everest Introduces Spices In Jar Packaging
2021
Everest Forays Into The Herbs And Seasonings Category With A New Brand Tasteeto As Well As Ginger Garlic Paste.
